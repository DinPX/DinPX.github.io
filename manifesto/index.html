Cyberspace Manifestov1.0

I keep seeing posts and videos about how internet used to be a fun place to hangout with, like how it was more lively and had personality back then, and over the years I feel the same thing. We have this problem of corporate platforms bombarded with ads and algorithms that cater to no one's taste and yet reels everyone back in.

Profile page customization is still there. You can set your profile picture, cover picture, and others like badges as much as you want but that doesn't give you much control how your page will look, navigate, and feel like overall. The background doesn't change, the layout is fixed, and much worse, not all contents in your feed will stay as some posts get deleted for different reasons.

You can set your feed to any interests you're into by having most algorithms see what you like and share but then again you have no total control over the contents you see on the feed as algorithms changes over time.

Also because of it, not all your posts will be visible to your mutuals unless they visit your profile page. Furthermore, it can't be helped at times to feel like posting for the sake of content and clicks, for increasing numbers, instead of sharing your creation just for the sake of it. Having your work be treated as a mere content and have its worth defined by numbers gives me the ick.

Personally I find it as people yearning to feel the same way they did in the past, all the nostalgia and niche that made the internet far from what it is today, but it's just hard to. The social media platforms make it difficult to be how it is in the old days: having the freedom of expression and identity and the feeling that you belong and in home‚ùö

With the common web platforms being owned by companies who hold so much power they can abuse to their benefit, and doesn't really care about its users, it's no surprise that over the years these the internet experience becomes much worse. They can just take away useful features that they don't like or put them behind a paywall, strip away users of freedom of speech (see Twitter), throw more ads on your face, steal more of your data, and much more.
